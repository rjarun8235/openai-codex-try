## Design notes

- **One font.** Keep titles and body the same typeface.
- **Dark background.** Cursor / Codex branding is dark; your demo switches from slide → dark IDE → dark terminal without jarring contrast shifts.
- **No stock photos.** No robots, no brains, no glowing laptops.
- **Font size ≥ 32pt body.** Back of the room needs to read without squinting.
- **No animations.** Every click should reveal *everything* on that slide.
- **No logos.** Not Codex's, not Philips', not OpenAI's. Your voice is the brand.

## What NOT to put on any slide

- Code snippets → show them in the IDE instead
- Architecture diagrams → they suggest complexity you don't want audiences to fear
- Pricing tables → answer in Q&A, don't lead with it
- Feature comparison matrices → those are sales decks, not training demos
- "Our vision" statements → audience tunes out
